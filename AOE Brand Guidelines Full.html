The Rule for Two Darks

Deep Teal is the everyday Shelby color — logo, headers, body text, signature brand moments.

Charcoal is special-use only. Premium offers, deep-dive decks, “we’re getting serious” moments. Never on the same slide as Deep Teal unless very intentional. Think of Deep Teal as the voice, Charcoal as the gravity.

Color Don'ts
No pure white — always Paper White
No pure black — always Deep Teal or Charcoal
No saturated brights outside the palette
No gradients (one exception: hero image overlays)
No metallic gold — Soft Peach does the warmth job
Section — 02

Typography.

Two fonts carry the entire system. Outfit (modern geometric sans) handles all heading and UI hierarchy. Zilla Slab (warm slab serif) carries the body and emotional emphasis through italic. Both are free on Google Fonts and render identically across Canva, web, slides, and PDFs.

The logo’s typography is locked. The high-contrast serif in “ART OF EASE” and the script “The” live inside the logo files only — never used for general type elsewhere.

How Each Font Carries Weight
Outfit does visual emphasis through weight — Black, ExtraBold, Bold all do the work that italic would do in a serif system.
Zilla Slab does emotional emphasis through italic — pull quotes, signature moments, single-word emphasis in body copy.
The tension between Outfit’s modern geometry and Zilla Slab’s editorial warmth IS the Editorial Edge signature.
Italic as Signature — Now in Body
The Art of Ease.

Italics live in Zilla Slab — for pull quotes, single-word emphasis in body copy, and the tagline treatment. Outfit doesn’t italicize; weight does its emphasis work.

No script or handwritten fonts — the logo's script is logo-only
No system fonts when Outfit/Zilla Slab are available
No all-caps body paragraphs (eyebrows and labels only)
No fonts under 14px on web, 18pt on slides

Section — 05

Voice.

Warm. Empowering. Clear. Calm. Inviting.

Shelby writes how she thinks — in spirals, with mid-sentence corrections, tangents she acknowledges, realizations happening live on the page. Holds contradictions without apology: systems nerd AND mystic, strategic architect AND 'I have 25+ unfinished spreadsheets.'

Cardinal rule one: Don’t flatten the weirdness. Run-on sentences, mid-thought pivots, ALL CAPS bursts, and ellipses are signal, not noise. Texture IS the gift.

Cardinal rule two: Don’t smooth into LLM defaults. The AI tendency is to regress toward generic, statistically common language. Shelby’s voice does the opposite — it gets more specific, more textured, more hers when it’s working. If a rewrite reads 'cleaner' but sounds like Wikipedia or ChatGPT, it’s worse, not better.

Signature Vocabulary
Thinking out loud: "I mean," "the point is," "but anyways," "for reals," "actually though.."
Excitement: "what-do-ya-know!!", "little ol' ME?!?!", "geesh," "what a time to be alive"
Brand & philosophy: "ease," "structure creates freedom," "energetic pivots," "breadcrumbs," "aligned," "beautiful systems," "creative chaos," "both/and"
Casual texture: "sooo," "kinda," "dang," "stinkin'," "lol" (used genuinely)
Hard Refusals
Corporate jargon: "synergy," "leverage," "optimize," "best practices"
Guru / hustle: "boss babe," "manifest your dreams," "grinding," "no days off"
Pressure tactics: "limited spots," "act now," countdown timers
Spiritual bypass: "trust the process," "everything happens for a reason"
Formal closings: "Best regards," "Sincerely"

Slide Templates — Approved Layouts
1. Title slide: Full-bleed Deep Teal or Paper White. Title: Outfit ExtraBold, 64-80pt. Subtitle: Outfit small caps. 60-70% negative space.
2. Section divider: Full-bleed Deep Teal or Charcoal. Section number: Outfit small caps. Section title: Outfit Bold 54-64pt Paper White.
3. Single-focus content slide: Paper background. Headline: Outfit Bold 44-54pt. Body: Zilla Slab 20-24pt, max 3-5 lines. One supporting visual.
4. Two-column comparison: Two equal panels, Slate Mist hairline divider. Labels in Outfit small caps. Outfit Bold for differentiators.
5. Quote slide: Golden Tan or Soft Sage background. Quote: Zilla Slab Italic Medium 40-54pt Deep Teal. Attribution: Outfit small caps. 60%+ negative space.
6. CTA slide: Paper background. Invitation: Zilla Slab Italic Medium 40-48pt. Body: Zilla Slab 18-20pt, invitational tone. Soft Peach accent.

Layout Don'ts
Never more than one display-weight headline per slide or card
Never less than 40% whitespace on any surface
Never center-align body copy (eyebrows and short hero lines only)
Never fewer than 3 colors on a complex surface — but never more than 4
Never crowd a CTA — give it its own breathing room
Never use the same background for 3+ slides in a row — vary the rhythm

Image-to-Text Ratio
Live pitch decks: 60% text-led, 30% mixed, 10% image-led. Audience is reading and listening.
Async PDF decks: 50% text-led, 35% mixed, 15% image-led. More room for visual storytelling.
Social carousels: 40% text-led, 40% mixed, 20% image-led. Stop the scroll.
Speaker Decks vs. Standalone
Speaker decks: Sparse. One word, one image, one number. The talk fills the gaps. Each slide = a punctuation mark.
Standalone decks: Self-explaining. More body copy. Captions. Clear reading order. Should make sense without you in the room.
Transitions
Default to no transitions or simple fades only. Editorial = clean cuts.
Never flips, spins, wipes, or 3D rotations. Never the dissolve. The eye should not have to work to follow the deck.
Slide Don'ts
Never put more than 5 bullet points on one slide
Never put both a chart AND a paragraph of body copy on the same slide
Never use bullet points and body paragraphs on the same slide
Never repeat the same slide layout 3+ slides in a row
Never use stock 'business' imagery (handshakes, salad-laugh, generic skylines)
Never put a CTA on a slide that has another competing focal point
Never use slide animations beyond simple fade

Master Cheatsheet.

Print this. Tape it next to your monitor. Send it to every team member, contractor, and AI tool. Everything here is non-negotiable unless explicitly broken with intention.

Top 10 DOs
Use Deep Teal (#2b5f5c) as primary on 60% of surfaces
Use Paper White (#fdfcf9) as default background — never pure white
Pair Outfit (headings, weight-driven) with Zilla Slab (body, italic-driven)
Use the logo's exact files — AOE primary, full wordmark, icon only
Write in Shelby's voice: warm, run-on, mid-sentence pivot, ALL CAPS bursts
Maintain 40-60% whitespace on every surface
One idea per slide. One CTA per page. One headline per card.
Use Outfit Bold/ExtraBold for visual hierarchy AND Zilla Slab Italic for emotional emphasis
Apply the 60/30/10 deck rhythm — 60% Paper / 20% Tan-Sage / 15% Teal-Charcoal / 5% full-image
Read it out loud — does it sound like Shelby would say it that way?
Top 10 DON'Ts
No pure white, no pure black — always Paper White and Deep Teal
No corporate jargon, guru-speak, hustle language, or AI vocabulary blacklist words
No script fonts, system fonts, or more than 2 typefaces total
No center-aligned body copy or all-caps body paragraphs

AI Pattern Refusals — The Statistical Tells
If AI-generated content reaches Shelby's hands with any of these patterns, it gets refused — regardless of whether the message is "right":
  • "delve / tapestry / showcase / underscore / pivotal / intricate / crucial"
  • "stands as / serves as / represents" (where "is" would work)
  • "-ing" analysis tails ("...contributing to the broader X")
  • "Not only X, but Y" negative parallelism
  • "Additionally,", "Furthermore,", "Moreover," paragraph-openers
  • "In conclusion / In summary / Overall" closers
  • Title Case Headers (except brand display moments)
  • Em dash overuse without the run-on cadence of Shelby's actual voice
  • Rule-of-three "elegant" listings ("clear, compelling, concise")
Common Mistakes by Asset Type
Slides
Cramming too much, using more than 2 fonts, center-aligned body, repetitive backgrounds, bullets-AND-body, transitions beyond fade.
PDFs
Dense pages with no whitespace, headers/body indistinguishable, line widths over 720px, no clear visual rhythm between sections.
Social
Carousels with two ideas per slide, hooks that sound polished/clean (vs. textured/Shelby), generic stock imagery, missing CTA slide.
Web
Multiple competing CTAs in one section, line widths too wide for reading, pure white backgrounds, navy/grey corporate UI fallback.
Voice & Copy
Over-smoothing run-ons, removing ALL CAPS bursts, sanitizing personality, AI vocabulary creeping in, ending sentences with "navigate" or "leverage".
Email
Formal closings ("Best regards"), too-clean structure, missing the warm-checking-in opener, no parenthetical asides, no genuine personality breaks.
The Litmus Test — Two Questions
Before publishing anything, ask:
1. Does this feel calm + clear + quietly confident?
If it feels frantic, busy, or sales-y — slow down. Strip a layer. Breathe more whitespace into it.
2. Does this sound like Shelby would say it out loud?
If it reads like Wikipedia, ChatGPT, or a corporate brochure — rewrite. The texture IS the gift.
